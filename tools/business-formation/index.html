LLC Formation Landscape:
Speed vs. Cost (2026)

Establishing a legal wall between personal assets and business liabilities. A comparative look at the pricing models, privacy features, and processing times of major filing services.

By The Editorial Team • Updated Jan 2026

For individuals recovering from financial hardship, the preservation of assets is paramount. When transitioning from a sole proprietorship to a formal entity, the **LLC (Limited Liability Company)** serves as a primary legal structure to separate personal savings from business liabilities.

This analysis categorizes the primary formation services available in 2026. Rather than ranking them subjectively, we break down their utility based on **initial filing costs**, **ongoing compliance fees**, and **privacy features** (such as Registered Agent inclusion) to help you match a service to your specific budget and privacy requirements.

This appeals to business owners who prioritize automated alerts over the lowest possible long-term cost.

3. Northwest Registered Agent

Focus: Privacy Protection

Northwest distinguishes itself through a "Privacy by Default" policy. Unlike other services that may list the business owner's home address on public state filings, Northwest uses its own corporate address for all client filings. This feature is particularly relevant for individuals seeking to minimize their public data footprint.

Key Specifications

  • ▪ Uses Northwest's address on public forms.
  • ▪ Explicit "No Data Selling" policy.
  • ▪ US-based "Corporate Guide" support model.

Trade-offs

Decision Framework: Assessing Costs

The Hidden Variable: Registered Agent Fees

Every LLC is legally required to designate a Registered Agent to receive government correspondence. This choice impacts long-term costs.

  • Included Bizee and Northwest include this service for the first year (valued at ~$100-$150), reducing the year-one total cost.
  • Add-on ZenBusiness and LegalZoom typically treat this as a paid add-on or inclusion only in higher-tier packages.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I start an LLC if I have a bankruptcy on record? ▼

Yes. Personal bankruptcy does not legally prohibit the formation of an LLC. However, it may impact the business's ability to secure lines of credit or loans in the early stages.

Do I need an EIN? ▼

Yes. An Employer Identification Number (EIN) functions as the tax ID for the business. It is required to open business bank accounts and separate business finances from personal funds.

Should I act as my own Registered Agent? ▼

While legal, it is often discouraged for privacy reasons. The address of the Registered Agent is published in state records. Using a third-party service ensures your home address remains private and prevents process servers from arriving at your residence.
