Emergency Mode

The Crisis Budget Worksheet

When the math doesn't work, you need a different kind of math. Prioritizing survival over solvency for the next 30 days.

Standard budgeting advice assumes you have enough income to cover your basic needs. But when you are in a true financial emergency—due to job loss, medical crisis, or imminent default—standard advice fails.

In this mode, you are not trying to "save for retirement." You are trying to survive the month without losing your home or your ability to work. This requires a ruthless prioritization method known as The Four Walls.

The Four Walls Concept

You must fund these four categories before you pay a single penny to anyone else (including credit cards):

  1. Food: Groceries only. No dining out.
  2. Utilities: Lights, heat, and water. Services required for habitability.
  3. Shelter: Rent or Mortgage. Staying housed is the priority.
  4. Transportation: Gas or bus fare required to get to your job/interviews.

The Rule: If a bill is not one of these walls, it gets paused until the crisis is over.

The Survival Audit

Expense Category Essential? (4 Walls) Monthly Cost "Slash" Potential

By pausing non-essentials, you reclaim $495 of cash flow to protect your housing.

The Hard Truth

The numbers in red above—Debt Minimums, Subscriptions, Dining Out—are often the hardest to cut emotionally. You may worry about your credit score dropping if you pause debt payments.

However, in a crisis, credit scores can be rebuilt; lost housing cannot. When you are bleeding cash, you must stop the bleeding before you worry about your reputation.
